<Section icon={Swords} title="The Mana Drain Mechanic & PvP Strategy">

What truly separates **clover retribution world tree magic** from other Mythics like Blood or Time is the Mana Drain passive. When an enemy is hit by specific "Root" abilities, they are encased in a greenish aura. While this aura is active, every subsequent hit from the World Tree user saps the opponent's mana pool.

In PvP, this is devastating. Most high-tier players rely on mana-heavy combos to maintain pressure. By consistently landing World's Root or Forest's Embrace, you can effectively "silence" your opponent by leaving them with zero resources to cast their own spells. 

| Ability | Mana Drain Active? | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **World's Root** | **Yes** | Mid-range poke and debuffing |
| **Forest's Embrace** | **Yes** | Escaping combos or finishing |
| **Verdant Vortex** | No | Shield pressure and DoT |
| **Arboreal Ascendancy** | No | Zoning and tethering |

<Callout type="warning" title="PvP Caution">
The mana drain only procs on hits while the debuff aura is active. If you miss your initial root, you lose the drain pressure. Practice your cursor placement to ensure the roots spawn directly under the target.
</Callout>

</Section>
