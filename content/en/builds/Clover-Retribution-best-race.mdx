<Section icon={Target} title="Choosing Your Race: A Step-by-Step Guide">
Selecting the optimal race involves more than just picking the highest-tier option. It requires careful consideration of your preferred playstyle, build goals, and resource availability. Follow these steps to make the best choice for your character.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Define Your Playstyle">
    Before rolling for a race, decide whether you want to focus on **magic damage, physical damage, tanking, healing, or utility**. This will narrow down your options significantly. For example, a pure magic user will prioritize INT and Mana buffs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Assess Race Rarity and Availability">
    Mythical races are powerful but incredibly rare. Be realistic about your chances of acquiring them. Legendary and Epic races often provide excellent value and are more accessible. You might start with a solid A-tier race and aim for an S-tier later.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Analyze Stat Bonuses and Traits">
    Examine the specific stat increases (STR, DEX, INT, CON, HP, Mana) and unique traits each race offers. Does it directly boost your primary damage type or enhance your survivability in a way that complements your build?
    <Callout type="info">
    Remember that some traits, like Frostward or Gold Fever, are situational. While useful, they might not outweigh core combat stats for a primary combat character.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Consider Synergy with Magic and Weapons">
    Your race should ideally enhance your chosen magic type (e.g., Dark Elf for Fire Magic) or weapon type (e.g., Gorgon for piercing weapons). Look for direct damage buffs or stat increases that amplify your main offensive or defensive capabilities.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Plan for Progression (If Applicable)">
    Some races, like Hybrid Dwarf, offer benefits for crafting or specific professions. If you plan to engage heavily in these activities, a utility race might be a good secondary character or a strategic choice if it aligns with your long-term goals.
  </Step>
</Steps>

</Section>
