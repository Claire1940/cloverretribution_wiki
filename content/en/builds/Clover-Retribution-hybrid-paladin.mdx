<Section icon={Target} title="Achieving Blessed State and Tier Progression">
The path to a fully optimized Hybrid Paladin involves two primary methods: collecting True Paladin Soul Fragments (TPFs) and crafting Graces. While both contribute to progression, TPFs are essential for achieving the Blessed state, a cornerstone of this race's power.

### True Paladin Soul Fragments (TPFs)

TPFs are the most reliable way to progress your Hybrid Paladin and become Blessed. Each TPF used has a 1% chance to instantly bless you. If you don't get lucky, using 20 TPFs guarantees the Blessed state as a pity system. TPFs also grant progression experience, with each TPF providing 4 experience points compared to Graces' 2.

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Note">
You **must have Hybrid Paladin equipped** for True Paladin Soul Fragments and other related items to drop.
</Callout>

**Farming TPFs:**
TPFs can be farmed from various enemies, with the Batlord offering a decent 18% drop rate, albeit with a 24-hour cooldown. Higher-tier Devils (Tier 3 and 4) and bosses like Atheon and Morning Star also drop them. While buying TPFs with Robux is an option, farming is generally recommended as they are not excessively difficult to obtain.

<Section icon={Shield} title="The Role of Graces in Hybrid Paladin">
Graces offer an alternative, albeit more challenging, method for Hybrid Paladin progression and provide valuable buffs. These items are crafted from specific catalysts dropped by various enemies. While the grind for Graces can be arduous, their unique buffs can significantly enhance your gameplay.

### Crafting Graces

You can craft Graces using the Hybrid Grace Weaving ability. Catalysts are dropped from enemies like Etherstone Golem (Gleaming Ether Core), Netherwing, Hamson, and Tier 2+ Devils. Combining multiple catalysts into a single Grace creates more powerful effects, but also lowers the success chance.

<Callout type="info">
The grace system is reportedly being changed to allow Graces to be cast as temporary buffs rather than consumable items, lasting for an hour. This could significantly impact their utility and progression value in the future.
</Callout>
