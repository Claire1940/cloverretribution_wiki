<Section icon={Target} title="Obtaining the Anti-Magic Devil Union">

Securing the Anti-Magic Devil is the most difficult step in finalizing your **Clover Retribution anti magic build**. Players must venture into Rifts to encounter these entities. The spawn rates are heavily weighted against the Anti-Magic type, making it a prestigious and rare acquisition.

| Devil Type | Spawn Chance | Primary Benefit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Hellfire Devil** | 60% | Hellflame Torrent (3k Damage) |
| **Cursed Devil** | 35% | Devil Union Form (No unique skills yet) |
| **Anti-Magic Devil** | 5% | **Anti-Magic Great Sword & Whirlwind** |

To optimize your chances, you can use an **Abyssal Relic** to reroll the devil type. However, the percentages remain the same, so expect to spend a significant amount of currency if your luck is low. Once you have the correct devil, you must build a relationship with it through meditation and specific quests.

| Quest Name | Relation Gain | Requirement |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mana Wisp Quest** | +5 Relation | Collect wisps in the overworld |
| **Eyeball Quest** | +10 to +15 Relation | Defeat specific mob types |
| **Devil Heart Quest** | +20 Relation | Rare drop from high-level bosses |
| **Devil Conversation** | Variable | Meditate for 10 minutes (N Key) |

<Callout type="warning" title="Bound vs Unbound">
Bound devils start at -20 relation, while unbound devils start at 0. This means bound devil users will need to put in significantly more work before they can begin Devil Training.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Binding Vows and Permanent Stat Scaling">

To truly maximize the **Clover Retribution anti magic build**, you must utilize Binding Vows. These are high-stakes deals made with your devil that offer permanent power at a significant cost. For Anti-Magic users, these vows are often the difference between a good build and a god-tier build.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Mana Exchange Vow">
    Available to all users. You exchange 10% of your total regular Mana for 10% more Devil Mana. Since Anti-Magic builds rarely use regular Mana, this is essentially a free buff to your Devil Union duration.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Arm Vow">
    Exclusive to Unbound Devil users. You sacrifice your character's arm to permanently gain 5% of your Devil's stats in your human form. This is a massive permanent boost to your base Strength and Speed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Spirit Rejection">
    Be aware that making a Binding Vow will cause Spirits to reject you for a set period. You cannot have more than one Binding Vow active at the same time, so choose the one that fits your playstyle best.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Vow Type | Requirement | Benefit | Drawback |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mana Trade** | Any Devil | +10% Devil Mana | -10% Regular Mana |
| **Stat Sacrifice** | Unbound Only | **+5% Permanent Stats** | Permanent loss of arm visual |

<Callout type="info" title="Visual Glitch Note">
The lost arm from the Binding Vow can sometimes bug out and show through armor. While it looks unique, it is a purely cosmetic drawback alongside the mechanical rejection from spirits.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Optimal Stat Allocation and Gear">

A **Clover Retribution anti magic build** is only as strong as the stats backing it up. Because you are likely running Divine Restriction, your stat points should be heavily focused on physical dominance rather than magical utility.

**Primary Stats for Anti-Magic:**
- **Strength**: Your number one priority. All Anti-Magic Great Sword skills scale with this.
- **Constitution**: Essential for surviving high-level rifts and the devil training fights.
- **Dexterity/Agility**: Helps with movement speed and attack animations, allowing for faster Whirlwind procs.

**Recommended Race/Trait Synergy:**
- **Divine Restriction**: The gold standard. It provides the highest physical damage ceiling.
- **Elder Vampire**: Provides the necessary regeneration to survive long battles without relying on healing magic.
- **High Elf**: While typically for mages, the increased mana pool can be converted via Binding Vows into massive Devil Mana reserves.
