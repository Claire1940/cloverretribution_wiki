<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prioritize Magic Damage Nodes">
    For most mage builds, focus on skill tree nodes that directly increase magic damage. These are often found in the "Int" skill tree branch. While some nodes offer mana cost reduction, pure damage is usually more impactful.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Invest in Specific Magic Branches">
    Magics like Light, Dark, Wind, Earth, Healing, Legion, and Shadow have dedicated skill trees. If you're using one of these, maxing out its specific branch is often the best course of action. For Light users, reducing cooldowns is crucial. Flame users benefit immensely from the Ryomen branch.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Consider Ethereion's Blessing">
    For mages not using Light magic, the Ethereion's Blessing branch offers significant magic damage boosts for a relatively cheap 14 skill points. Accessing this branch requires receiving Ethereion's blessing from a spirit rift. This can be a game-changer for many builds.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Avoid Non-Essential Nodes">
    Nodes like "Broom Boost" or movement speed buffs often provide minimal returns for valuable skill points. Similarly, some "Strength" branch nodes might not be optimal for magic users. Be selective and prioritize nodes that directly enhance your magic or survivability.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning">
Skill points are currently not resettable in Clover Retribution. Make your choices carefully, as a poor allocation can severely hinder your build. Fabio has hinted at a reset option in the future, but for now, every point counts.
</Callout>
