<Section icon={Pickaxe} title="The Node Cycling Strategy">

One of the most common mistakes new players make when searching for **clover retribution copper** is simply walking through a cave and leaving if they don't see the orange-tinted nodes immediately. Copper and Iron share spawn points with common Stone. If the cave is full of Stone, no Copper will ever appear.

To maximize your yield, you must engage in "Node Cycling." This involves destroying every node you see, regardless of whether it contains the material you want. By clearing the Stone, you force the game to roll for a new spawn in that location, which has a chance to be Copper.

| Node Type | Appearance | Rarity | Primary Use |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stone Node** | Grey, jagged | **Common** | Basic building, clearing space |
| **Copper Node** | Orange/Brown veins | **Rare** | Weapon crafting, armor plating |
| **Iron Node** | Metallic silver veins | **Rare** | High-tier weaponry, tools |

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Clear the Area">
    Enter the cave and eliminate any nearby Bandits or hostile mobs to ensure you can mine undisturbed.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Destroy All Stone">
    Use your pickaxe to break every grey Stone node in the vicinity. Do not leave them standing.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Wait for Respawns">
    Nodes typically respawn every few minutes. Move to a different section of the cave and repeat the process.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Collect Copper">
    Return to the original spots. If a node spawns with orange veins, mine it immediately to secure your copper.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Spawn Mechanics">
Copper has a significantly lower spawn rate than Stone. You may need to clear 10-15 Stone nodes before a single Copper node appears. Persistence is the key to a successful farm.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Target} title="Maximizing Farming Efficiency">

To get the most **clover retribution copper** in the shortest amount of time, you need to optimize your route and your gear. Professional farmers don't just wander; they follow a strict circuit that minimizes downtime and maximizes node interaction.

| Efficiency Factor | Impact | Recommendation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tool Tier** | **High** | Upgrade to Iron Pickaxe ASAP |
| **Server Hopping** | **Medium** | Switch servers if a cave is empty |
| **Time of Day** | **Low** | Night cycles have fewer players |
| **Buffs/Potions** | **Medium** | Use Luck potions if available |

**Farming Optimization Steps:**
1. **Empty Inventory**: Sell or stash all unnecessary items before heading to the Grasslands.
2. **Set a Spawn Point**: Use a nearby waypoint or bed to ensure a quick return if you fall in combat.
3. **The Loop**: Start at the cave entrance, clear the left wing, move to the central chamber, then the right wing. By the time you finish the right wing, the left wing nodes should be respawning.
4. **Ignore Junk**: If your inventory fills up, drop the basic Stone. It has very little value compared to the copper you are hunting.

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Pro-Tip">
If you are playing with a friend, have one person focus on killing the NPCs while the other focuses exclusively on mining. This prevents the "interrupted mining" animation and speeds up the cycle significantly.
</Callout>
