<Section icon={Swords} title="Yeti Boss: Location and Combat Mechanics">

To obtain the **clover retribution yeti staff**, you must venture into the Tundra and face the Yeti. This mob is notorious for its high health pool and crowd-control abilities that can easily wipe an unprepared party. The Yeti functions in three distinct attack phases, each requiring specific movement patterns to avoid being stunned or crushed.

**Yeti Attack Patterns:**
- **Yeti Roar**: A massive AoE shout that causes stun and damage. This is the most dangerous move for Mages as it interrupts spellcasting.
- **Yeti Kick**: A semi-AoE physical strike that knocks players back.
- **Yeti Slam**: A ground-pound move that deals heavy AoE damage in a circle around the boss.

<Callout type="warning" title="Tundra Hazard">

<Section icon={Target} title="The Yeti Boss Cheese Strategy">

If you are struggling to defeat the boss legitimately, the community has discovered a "Cheese" method that allows you to farm the **clover retribution yeti staff** with minimal risk. This requires at least two players: one to act as the "Leash" (Tank) and one to act as the "DPS" (Mage).

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Aggro the Boss">
    Have one player (the Leash) attack the Yeti to gain aggro. The Leash should lead the Yeti toward the front wall of the arena.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Wall Hug">
    The Leash player must stand against the wall and hold their block (F). If positioned correctly, the Yeti will "hug" the wall while trying to reach the player, effectively locking its movement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Back-Shot DPS">
    The second player (the Mage) should position themselves directly behind the Yeti. From this position, you can spam spells and M1 attacks with the staff without triggering the Yeti's frontal attacks.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Manage the Roar">
    Even when cheesed, the Yeti will still Roar. Both players must keep their block ready. Once the Yeti's health is low, the Leash player can join in for the final kill to maximize loot efficiency.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Efficiency Tip">
Using this cheese method reduces the time per kill by approximately 40%, making the 0.7% drop rate for the staff much more manageable over long farming sessions.
