<Section icon={Target} title="Step-by-Step: How to Join a Party">
Joining a party in Clover Retribution is a straightforward process. Whether you're inviting friends or looking to team up with random players, the in-game system makes it easy.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate the Player">
    To invite someone to your party, you first need to be near them in the game world. You can either walk up to their character or locate them on the player list if they are in your server.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Open the Interaction Menu">
    Once near the player, click on their character. This should bring up an interaction menu. Alternatively, you can often find a "Players" list or "Social" tab in your game interface that shows all players in your current server.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Send a Party Invite">
    From the interaction menu or player list, look for an option like "Invite to Party" or "Send Party Request." Click on this option to send an invitation to the other player.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Accepting an Invite">
    If someone invites you, a notification will typically appear on your screen. You will have the option to "Accept" or "Decline" the invitation. Accepting will instantly add you to their party.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Confirm Party Formation">
    Once the invitation is accepted, you should see a party UI element appear on your screen, showing the health bars and names of your party members. You are now officially part of the group!
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Important Note on Devil Union">
Some specific mechanics, such as progressing your Devil Union for certain types of devils (e.g., Arrogant Devil), explicitly state that you **cannot be in a party** and must kill bosses alone to gain Union progression. Always check the requirements for unique character progressions.
</Callout>

### Effective Party Communication

Good communication is the backbone of any successful party. Use the in-game chat or external voice chat (like Discord) to coordinate.

| Communication Aspect | Importance                                                                  | Example                                                                          |
|----------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Role Assignment**  | Clearly define who will tank, heal, or deal damage.                         | "I'll tank the Golem, you guys focus on damage."                                  |
| **Target Focus**     | Decide which enemy to attack first, especially in multi-enemy encounters.     | "Let's take down the Wind Monks first, then the Ogre."                             |
| **Ability Cooldowns**| Inform teammates about important ability cooldowns (e.g., healing spells). | "My heal is on cooldown for 10 seconds, watch your health."                        |
| **Movement & Positioning** | Coordinate movement during boss mechanics to avoid damage.                  | "Stack up on me for the AoE heal!" or "Spread out for the incoming attack!"       |
| **Loot Distribution**| Agree on how to handle loot drops (e.g., "Need before Greed" or free-for-all). | "Anyone need the Corrupted Mana Crystal? Otherwise, I'll take it."              |
