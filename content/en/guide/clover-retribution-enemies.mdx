The Aetherstone Golem serves as the final encounter in the Aetherstone Isle raid, the first of seven major raids currently available in the game. The Golem is entirely immune to poison status effects, rendering many mage builds less effective if they rely on damage-over-time poison spells. Every attack the Golem performs deals bludgeoning damage, which can be mitigated through specific armor sets or defensive buffs.

| Attack Name | Description | Counter Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Stone Fist** | Summons a stone fist from the ground beneath the player. | Perfect dodge or parry when the ground glows. |
| **Ground Slam** | A heavy downward strike dealing massive AoE damage. | Jump or time a parry to negate the impact. |
| **Spinning AoE** | The Golem spins rapidly, hitting all nearby players. | Maintain distance or use a perfect dodge. |
| **Crystal DPS Check** | Six purple crystals appear when the boss is stunned. | Destroy all 6 crystals within 30 seconds. |

<Callout type="warning" title="Raid Failure Warning">
Failure to complete the DPS check results in an instant kill for everyone in the raid area. If the raid fails, the portal to Aetherstone Isle enters a 15-minute cooldown before it can be reopened.

<Section icon={Map} title="Rune Farming and World Bosses">

To even face the high-tier **clover retribution enemies** in raids, you must first farm specific world bosses for entry materials. The Aetherstone Isle portal, located south of the Unnamed Village at the center of the lake, requires four distinct runes. These runes are not guaranteed drops and require significant farming.

The runes—Wrath, Challenge, Earth, and Tempest—are obtained from the chests of event and world bosses. Based on community data, the drop rate for these runes sits between 30% and 40% per chest.

| Boss Name | Primary Location | Key Rune Drops |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bandit Lord** | Grasslands Bandit Camp | Wrath, Earth |
| **Warlord** | Desert Outpost | Challenge, Tempest |
| **Camson** | Hidden Forest | Random Rune |
| **Lucius** | Ancient Ruins | Random Rune |

<Callout type="tip" title="Efficiency Tip">
It is standard community practice to insert the **Wrath Rune** last into the pillars. This ensures that all party members have enough time to reach the portal before it closes, as the portal activation is triggered by the final rune placement.
</Callout>
