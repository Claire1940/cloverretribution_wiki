<Section icon={MousePointer} title="Step-by-Step: How to Use Fast Travel">

Walking across the map can be time-consuming, even with a broom. Fortunately, Clover Retribution features a fast travel system managed by an NPC named **Dory**. Using Dory allows you to jump between discovered villages instantly, though it will cost you a bit of gold.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Locate Dory">
    Find Dory in major settlements. He is usually standing near the entrance of the Unnamed Village or the central hub of the Clover Kingdom.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Check Your Gold">
    Teleportation costs vary based on distance. Ensure you have at least 500-1000 gold before attempting a long-distance jump.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Select Destination">
    Interact with Dory and choose your destination from the dialogue menu. You can only travel to places you have previously visited on foot.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Confirm Map Location">
    Once you arrive, open your map (;) to re-orient yourself and find the nearest quest markers or daily objectives.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Teleportation Costs">
Teleportation prices are dynamic. Traveling from the Unnamed Village to the Desert is significantly more expensive than traveling to the nearby Clover Kingdom.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Smartphone} title="Mobile vs. PC Navigation Experience">

Clover Retribution is fully mobile-enabled, but the UI differs significantly from the PC version. While PC users rely on the semicolon key, mobile players have a more visual interface.

| Feature | PC Experience | Mobile Experience |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Map Access** | Semicolon (;) or M Menu | Dedicated Screen Button |
| **Movement** | WASD Keys | On-screen Joystick |
| **Combat** | Mouse L/R Click | Action Buttons (Bottom Right) |

| **Camera** | Mouse Movement | Swipe Screen |

While the keyboard offers an unrestricted view and faster hotkey access, the mobile version includes all major commands as visible buttons. If your map button is missing on mobile, check your settings to ensure the UI scale isn't hiding the sidebar.

<Section icon={Target} title="Finding Hidden Map Locations: Aetherstone Isle">

Some locations don't appear on the map until you perform specific actions. Aetherstone Isle, the site of the first major raid, is a prime example. To open the portal to this location, you must interact with stone pillars scattered across the map.

**Rune Locations for Map Completion:**
- **Wrath Rune**: Obtained from the Bandit Lord or Warlord.
- **Earth Rune**: Found in the chests of event bosses like Lucius.
- **Tempest Rune**: Dropped by high-level event bosses.
- **Challenge Rune**: Rare drop from Crimson or Warlord chests.

| Rune Type | Pillar Location | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Wrath** | South of Unnamed Village | Insert this rune **last** to keep the portal open |
| **Earth** | Near the Tundra Entrance | Guarded by high-level NPCs |
| **Tempest** | Clover Kingdom Outskirts | Requires flight to reach easily |
| **Challenge** | Desert Border | High-threat area with Scorpions |

<Callout type="warning" title="Raid Readiness">
Opening the map to Aetherstone Isle is only half the battle. You need a team of at least 4-5 players to pass the **DPS check** (destroying 6 purple crystals in 30 seconds), or the raid will fail and go on a 15-minute cooldown.
</Callout>
