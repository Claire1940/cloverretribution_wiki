<Section icon={Swords} title="Combat and Questing Fundamentals">
Combat in _Clover Retribution_ combines action with strategic RPG elements. Equipping your chosen weapon or magic is the first step. Melee combat involves basic left-click attacks for combos, while magic uses equipped spells on your toolbar, each with a cooldown. Efficiently managing your attacks and understanding enemy patterns will be key to success.

### Quest Types and Their Benefits

Quests are the primary method for leveling up and progressing in _Clover Retribution_. There are three main types, each offering unique benefits:

| Quest Type       | Icon              | Description                                                               | Leveling Efficiency |
| :--------------- | :---------------- | :------------------------------------------------------------------------ | :------------------ |
| **Story Quests** | Gold Exclamation  | Progresses the main narrative, often tied to boss battles.                | Moderate            |
| **Repeatable Quests** | Blue Exclamation | Can be completed multiple times, excellent for grinding XP.               | **High**            |
| **Daily Quests** | Green Exclamation | Completable once per day, offers good XP and other valuable rewards.      | High (daily limit)  |

<Callout type="success" title="Efficient Leveling Strategy">

<Section icon={Gem} title="Understanding Your Stats and Builds">
Every time you level up in _Clover Retribution_, you gain a single point to allocate to one of four core stats. Understanding these stats is crucial for developing an effective character build.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="Dexterity (DEX)">
    - Increases **slashing and piercing damage** (swords, daggers, bows).
    - Enhances the **perfect dodge window**.
    - Ideal for agile, weapon-focused builds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Strength (STR)">
    - Increases **bludgeoning damage** (hammers, cestuses, punches).
    - Boosts **block damage reduction**.
    - Allows lifting heavy objects.
    - Favored by tanky or heavy-hitting melee builds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Intelligence (INT)">
    - Increases **magical damage** from spells.
    - Accelerates **spell learning**.
    - Essential for mage builds.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Constitution (CON)">
    - Increases **health and mana**.
    - Improves **resilience to conditions** like "envenomed."
    - Important for all builds, especially tanks.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

<Callout type="warning" title="Stat Allocation Caution">
Avoid rushing into a specific build early on. Experiment with different weapon types and magic to discover your preferred playstyle. **Stat resets are rare and often cost Robux**, so thoughtful allocation is key.
</Callout>

While YouTube guides might offer "optimal" builds, it's often more beneficial for new players to experience the different playstyles offered by strength, dexterity, and magic-based approaches. This allows you to naturally gravitate towards a build that suits you, preventing wasted time and potential Robux on stat resets.

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Crafting and Gear Acquisition">
Crafting is a cornerstone of gear acquisition in _Clover Retribution_, offering some of the best equipment available outside of boss drops. The process is straightforward:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Gather Materials">
    Collect necessary materials from around the world (e.g., copper, iron, stone), from defeated mobs, or by purchasing from shopkeepers. Bosses are excellent sources for rare crafting components.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Visit the Blacksmith">
    Locate the Blacksmith NPC in town. They are your go-to for all crafting needs.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Craft Your Item">
    Select the item you wish to craft. The confirmation screen will display the crafting success chance. Low-level items often have 100% success, but higher-tier gear can have lower odds. Pay the required fee to attempt the craft.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="info" title="Artifact Quality Weapons">
When crafting your starter weapon, aim for **artifact quality**. This provides a free 1.5x damage multiplier, significantly boosting your early combat effectiveness. Craft multiple until you get one if resources allow.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Maximizing Gains with AFK Farming and Dailies">
Even when you're not actively playing, _Clover Retribution_ offers ways to progress. The "Library" game mode is designed for AFK farming, allowing you to passively earn crucial resources.

### The Library: Your AFK Hub

The Library provides:
- **Magic Spins**: Reroll your magic type to find more powerful or desired spells.
- **Race Spins**: Change your race, which can significantly impact your stats and build potential (e.g., Orcs for Strength/Constitution).
- **Trait Spins**: Acquire new traits that offer various bonuses, further customizing your character.

<Callout type="tip" title="Free Rerolls">
Utilize AFK farming in The Library to accumulate free magic, race, and trait spins. This allows you to optimize your character without spending Robux on rerolls, making it a valuable long-term strategy.
</Callout>

### Daily Quests: Consistent Rewards

Daily quests, marked with a green exclamation point on your minimap, are completable once per day and offer excellent experience and other rewards. Make it a habit to complete these whenever possible. Examples include:

| **Cat Rescue** | Tundra Village      | Find Whiskers the cat                     | 2 free race, trait, magic spins            | Very easy, good for early spins.            |
| **Scorpion Hunt** | Desert Village NPC  | Kill 5 Scorpions, collect 10 glands       | 4500 XP                                    | Good mid-game XP.                           |

### Golem and Beyond

The Golem on Aetherstone Isle is a significant mid-to-late game challenge. To summon the Golem, you need to activate four stone pillars with specific runes: Wrath, Challenge, Earth, and Tempest. These runes are typically dropped by various bosses like the Bandit Lord, Warlord, Campson, and Lucius.
