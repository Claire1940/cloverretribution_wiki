In Clover Retribution, spells are intrinsically linked to your grimoire, which essentially dictates your magic type. Obtaining a grimoire is the foundational step to unlocking any spell in the game. Without a grimoire, you won't have access to any magical abilities.

Grimoires are categorized by rarity, influencing the power and uniqueness of their associated spells. The game offers a variety of magic types, each with its own set of abilities that scale with your Intelligence (INT) stat.

### Grimoire Rarity and Types
Grimoires can be obtained through a spinning system on the game's home page. Each spin has a chance to grant you a grimoire of varying rarity.

| Rarity      | Example Magic Types | Chance | Notes                                     |
|-------------|---------------------|--------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Common**  | Healing, Melody     | High   | Basic spells, good for beginners.         |
| **Uncommon**| Earth, Wind         | Medium | More versatile abilities.                 |
| **Rare**    | Slime, Dice         | Low    | Unique effects and moderate power.        |
| **Epic**    | Lightning, Flame    | Very Low| Powerful, often with AOE capabilities.    |
| **Legendary**| Void, Water         | Ultra Low| Exceptional power, game-changing spells.  |
| **Mythical**| World Tree, Time    | Extremely Low| Top-tier, often with exclusive 4-Leaf moves.|

<Callout type="tip" title="Maximizing Your Grimoire Spins">
While spinning is based on luck, consider saving up spins if possible to increase your chances of getting a higher rarity grimoire. Early on, any grimoire will suffice to start your magical journey.
</Callout>

### 4-Leaf Grimoires
There's a rare 1% chance to obtain a grimoire with '4 Leaf' in its name. These special grimoires unlock exclusive 4-Leaf moves, significantly enhancing their power and utility. However, this special status does not save between spins unless you own the **Clover Storage gamepass**.

</Section>

<Section icon={Trophy} title="Efficient Spell Mastery Farming">
Farming spell mastery can be a time-consuming process, but with the right methods, you can significantly speed it up. The core idea is to repeatedly use your spells to gain mastery points. For optimal efficiency, many players utilize automation tools.

### Automated Spell Mastery with TinyTask
One of the most effective ways to farm spell mastery, especially for AFK grinding, is by using a macro program like **TinyTask**. This tool records your actions and can play them back continuously, allowing you to spam your spells without manual input.

<Callout type="info">
TinyTask is a legitimate macro tool and does not inherently contain viruses. Always download software from official or reputable sources to ensure safety.
</Callout>

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Prepare Your Character">
    Ensure your grimoire is equipped. Position your character in a safe, isolated area where you won't be disturbed or attacked. Make sure you have sufficient mana regeneration or a plan to reset regularly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Record Your Macro">
    Open TinyTask. Start recording.
    1. Open your grimoire.
    2. Use your highest mana cost spell.
    3. Use your other spells in sequence.
    4. Ensure all spells are used before their cooldowns reset.
    5. If you have low mana (e.g., due to Heavenly Restriction), wait for mana to regenerate sufficiently before using the next high-cost spell.
    6. Once all spells are on cooldown or you've used them all, perform a character reset (e.g., in-game reset or a quick teleport if applicable).
    Stop recording.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Configure Playback">
    In TinyTask, set the playback to "Continuous" to loop your recorded actions indefinitely.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Start AFK Farming">
    Start the macro. Your character will now repeatedly use spells and reset, accumulating spell mastery over time. This method can significantly reduce the time needed to max out your spell mastery, potentially achieving full mastery within a couple of days even with breaks.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="tip" title="Mana Management for Macros">
If your character has limited mana, ensure your macro accounts for mana regeneration time. You might need to adjust the timing between spell casts or include a brief pause before resetting to allow mana to recover.
</Callout>

</Section>

### Stat Allocation for Mages
Prioritizing Intelligence (INT) is key for any magic-focused build. This stat directly enhances your spell damage and often your mana pool.

| Stat             | Impact on Magic User                                  | Recommendation                                                              |
|------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Intelligence (INT)** | Increases spell damage, potentially mana capacity.    | **Primary Focus:** Allocate the majority of your stat points here.             |
| **Constitution (CON)** | Increases health and defense.                         | **Secondary Focus:** Allocate enough points for survivability, especially early on. |
| **Dexterity (DEX)**    | Increases melee damage, not directly beneficial for pure mages. | **Avoid:** Do not invest points unless you're building a hybrid character.       |
