<Section icon={Trophy} title="Unlocking Legendary Traits with Stat Allocation">
One of the most impactful aspects of Clover Retribution stats is the ability to unlock powerful Legendary Traits. These traits provide significant bonuses that can define your character's role and enhance your chosen playstyle. To unlock a Legendary Trait, you must allocate 60% or more of your total stat points into a single primary stat. It's important to note that only directly assigned stat points contribute to this 60% threshold; bonuses from armor, accessories, or your race do not count towards unlocking these traits.

| Stat Focus   | Legendary Trait      | Primary Effects                                     | Strategic Advantage                                  |
|:-------------|:---------------------|:----------------------------------------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------|
| **Dexterity**| Swift Reflexes       | Alters player's dash, +0.125 perfect dodge, +5 Speed | Enhanced agility, survivability through evasion      |
| **Strength** | Warrior's Might      | x2 Blocking DMG                                     | Superior defense, counter-attack potential           |
| **Intelligence**| Arcane Insight      | Magic DMG boost (up to 25% under 50% Mana), triple EXP for Clover Mastery | High burst damage, faster magic progression          |
| **Constitution**| Unyielding Sentinel | 5% Passive Defense (increases to 25% under 50% HP) | Exceptional tankiness, resilient in critical moments |

Choosing which stat to heavily invest in depends entirely on your desired combat role and preferred magic or weapon type. For instance, an Arcane build would prioritize Intelligence to unlock Arcane Insight, while a defensive character would aim for Unyielding Sentinel through Constitution.

<Callout type="warning">
While focusing 60% of your points into one stat is powerful, be mindful of neglecting other essential stats. A purely offensive build might struggle with survivability if Constitution is too low, or a tank might lack damage if Strength or Intelligence is ignored.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Swords} title="Optimizing Stat Caps for Magic Builds">
Beyond simply allocating points, understanding stat caps for your specific magic is crucial for maximizing your damage in Clover Retribution. Each magic ability has a soft cap, meaning that while you can invest more points beyond it, the damage increase becomes less efficient. For example, some lightning magic abilities might have a soft cap around 75 Intelligence. Investing 75 points would grant maximum value for that ability, with diminishing returns for points beyond that.

However, some magic types, like Dark Magic, feature extremely high stat caps (e.g., 1200, 2700 Intelligence for certain abilities). In such cases, it's often beneficial to pour almost all your stat points into Intelligence, as you're unlikely to hit the cap for all abilities, ensuring every point contributes significantly to damage.

| Magic Type   | Example Ability | Typical Soft Cap (INT) | Optimization Strategy                                      |
|:-------------|:----------------|:-----------------------|:-----------------------------------------------------------|
| **Lightning**| Lightning Circus| ~75                    | Aim for cap on key abilities, then diversify or increase cap via race. |
| **Wind**     | Tornado Blast   | Varies, often moderate | Balance between cap and overall build utility.             |
| **Dark**     | Shadow Coffin   | ~1200-2700             | Maximize Intelligence; unlikely to hit all caps, high returns. |
| **Fire**     | Fireball Barrage| Varies, often moderate | Focus on primary damage stat, consider hybrid if needed.    |

<Callout type="info">
Certain races, such as Hybrid Paladin, can increase the soft cap of abilities by a percentage (e.g., +10%). This allows for even higher damage potential for specialized builds.
</Callout>

A common strategy for many players is the "60/40 rule," where 60% of stat points go into your primary damage stat (Intelligence for mages, Strength for warriors) and 40% into Constitution for survivability. This provides a balanced approach, ensuring both damage output and resilience. However, for magics with exceptionally high stat caps, a full investment into the primary damage stat might be more effective.

</Section>

Here's a breakdown of the available methods for resetting your stat points:

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Wizard King Reset">
    If you've reached the rank of Intermediate Magic Knight 4 or higher, you can visit the Wizard King. This NPC offers an option to reset your skill tree, which effectively allows you to reallocate your stat points. This is often the easiest and most accessible method for high-ranking players.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Elixir of Reflection (Potion Maker)">
    For a more grind-oriented, free-to-play option, you can craft an **Elixir of Reflection** at the Potion Maker once you reach level 15. This potion requires a variety of ingredients, often obtained from mission board orders and boss drops. While it costs a substantial amount of Gold ($62,600) and specific materials, it guarantees a stat reset.
    <Callout type="tip">
    The Elixir of Reflection requires rare components like Spirit Feathers, Runes of Shattered Earth, Devil Horns, and more. Focus on completing mission board orders and defeating bosses to gather these materials efficiently.
    </Callout>
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Robux Purchase">
    For players seeking an immediate solution without the grind, a skill tree reset can be purchased for 750 Robux. This option provides instant reallocation of your stat points, ideal for quick build changes or rectifying errors.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Outfit Changes (Partial Reset)">
    While not a full stat reset, remember that stats gained from armor and accessories can be changed simply by swapping out your gear. This allows for quick adjustments to your build without affecting your core allocated stat points.
  </Step>
</Steps>
